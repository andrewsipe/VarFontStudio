What changed from the first pass, and why

  • Single column, not before/after. The change-type tag (renamed / ID reflowed / protected / same …) plus the color gutter now carries "what kind of change," and a small "was …" line underneath carries "what it used to be" only when that's useful — for an ID reflow, that's often just the old nameID, not a whole duplicated string.
  • "ID reflowed" vs "Renamed" are kept distinct since they mean different things for your two core features: reflow is the table restructuring (a string moved to a new, consistent nameID slot) with the text often untouched, while renamed means the text itself changed. Collapsing those into one "modified" bucket, like my first pass did, hid that distinction.
  • Protected OT rows get a caption, not a diff arrow — since they can't change, showing them as "same" would bury the fact that they're structurally exempt (e.g. legacy nameID 1/2 kept for OT compliance) rather than just untouched by coincidence.
  • Horizontal tabs replace the sidebar — STAT / fvar / name, each labeled with "changed of total" (e.g. "212 of 228"). Only the active tab's row list is in the DOM/List at a time, which is the main perf lever for large files: SwiftUI only builds and lays out one table's rows instead of three stacked Lists.
  • Streamlined switch hides "Same" rows table-wide. Legend chips are click-to-filter and stack with Streamlined — e.g. turn on Streamlined, then click "ID reflowed" alone to review just the 212 reflow moves in the name table without the renamed/protected rows in between.
